Hello, I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Michigan State University. My research field is the assurance and robustness of learning enabled systems (AI/ML powered systems). I apply a number of disciplines, such as evolutionary computing (e.g., novelty search), non-cooperative game theory, reinforcement learning, and other related techniques to address / improve the robustness of learning-enabled systems.

Research Interest

Machine Learning (DNN)

Software / Requirements Engineering

Evolutionary Computing

I use evolutionary computing techniques to address the robustness of ML / DNN components in safety-critical software (i.e., those that can have real-world consequences when they fail). Specifically, we aim to develop robust learning enabled systems do not fail when facing data that deviate from the expected distribution(s). Furthermore, we design alternate fail-safe capabilities to ensure that if the learning-enable system fails, it will fail in a safe manner.

Applications

Autonomous driving

Self-Adaptive Systems (SAS)

Data

As the capabilities and applications of DNNs are impressive and revolutionary, we are beginning to see more and more safety-critical systems rely on DNNs for decision making. I am interested in technology that can ensure that DNNs can be trusted.
